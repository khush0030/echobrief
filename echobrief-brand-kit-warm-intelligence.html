04 · Iconography

Icon Style

Icons use a consistent 2px stroke weight, rounded linecaps, and 24×24px grid. Keep icons warm and functional — never decorative for its own sake. Use Ember for active/featured icons; Ash for idle UI icons.

Icon Usage Rules
✓ Do
  • →Use Ember colour on active/primary icons
  • →Use Ash colour on idle/secondary icons
  • →Keep stroke weight consistent at 2px
  • →Use rounded linecaps and linejoins
  • →Pair icons with text labels in UI
✗ Don't
  • →Mix stroke weights (no 1px or 3px)
  • →Use filled icons (outline only)
  • →Use icons below 16px without simplification
  • →Use icons as pure decoration (no meaning)

08 · Brand Voice

Voice & Tone

EchoBrief writes like a brilliant, warm colleague — not a corporate machine. Direct, human, and always on the side of the user. Never jargony, never cold.

Warm
Like a trusted team member who genuinely cares. We acknowledge the real experience of meetings — messy, multilingual, and full of ideas.
  • "Every word captured — even in Hindi."
  • "Your team's ideas, never lost again."
  • "We got everything. You focus on the conversation."
Precise
We don't oversell. We tell you exactly what EchoBrief does — and we mean it. No vague AI promises.
  • "Auto-detected: Hindi, English, Tamil."
  • "3 action items. 1 decision. 34 minutes saved."
  • "Summary generated from 6 speakers."
Inclusive
We're built for how India actually works — code-switching, multiple languages in one call, regional accents. That's a feature, not an edge case.
  • "हिंदी, English, Tamil — your team's language."
  • "Built for the way India meets."
  • "No accent left behind."
Confident, not cocky
We believe in what we've built, but we don't shout about it. The product speaks. Copy should feel assured, not desperate.
  • "Try it free." (not "🚀🔥 GAME CHANGING AI")
  • "We detect what others miss."
  • "Your brief is ready." (not "AI-powered insights generated!")

09 · Motion & Animation

Motion Principles

Motion should feel like a warm, unhurried intelligence — not a frantic startup. Ease-out for entrances, ease-in for exits, and gentle spring for interactive responses.

Entrance Easing
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
Fast start, gently decelerates to rest. Used for cards, modals, toast notifications entering the screen.
transition: all 0.4s cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
Micro-interaction
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)
Standard ease-in-out for hover states, button responses, toggle animations. Duration: 150–200ms.
transition: all 0.18s cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)
Duration Scale
50ms → 500ms
50ms — icon swaps
150ms — hover states
250ms — state changes
400ms — page transitions
Waveform — Live Recording
Each bar animates independently with staggered delays (0–0.35s) and durations (0.5–1s) for organic, breathing feel.
Recording
Motion Philosophy
✓ Principles
  • →Motion has purpose — it communicates state
  • →Entrance animations build trust
  • →Loading states use the waveform pattern
  • →Respect prefers-reduced-motion
✗ Avoid
  • →Bouncy spring on serious data (summaries, transcripts)
  • →Animations faster than 50ms or slower than 500ms
  • →Looping animations on static content
  • →Scale/bounce on navigation or sidebars
